Food and Drinks

NIKUGEN Akasaka

Come enjoy Asian barbecued meat in the lively Roppongi area. At Nikugen, meat is everything. The beef is carefully selected and wet-aged for over 30 days to give diners the tastiest experience. The wet-aging process allows the meat to get more flavourful and tender. The star of Nikugen is the Black Angus beef. If you are not familiar with this beef, you should know that it is the breed that develops better marbling than most cattle. The marbling is known to keep the meat moist while cooking. If this didn’t not seduce you, the wine selection surely will. A wine cellar awaits at the entrance of the restaurant with over 60 varieties.

The open interior with snug sofas, pillows, bench seats and contemporary paintings all over the restaurant are the perfect ambiance for a relaxing, chic dining experience suitable for any occasion. Make sure to visit Nikugen for a delicious wining and dining time.

Asian Stall Village

If you have ever hesitated between having Indian, Chinese or Thai for dinner, the Asian Stall Village is there to please you! Opened in 2018 and located in the heart of what locals call Korean town, in Shin-Okubo, this similarly and colorful restaurant Can offer you dishes from 8 Asian countries! Diners don’t need to choose, the Asian Stall Village has prepared it all. Get comfortable and let yourself travel with their international courses offering food from Nepal, India, Vietnam, Thailand, Singapore, Malaysia , Korea and China.

The exotic atmosphere, with wooden decorations and tropical plants will surely remind you of a resort holiday in Southeast Asia. The bar area offers beers and drinks from all over Asia. The 150-seat restaurant is ideal for celebrations, gathering and parties, and the Asian Stall Village can provide a sound system, original decorations, free Wi-Fi, karaoke, projectors and more for your entertainment. Parties can be tailored to budget and your needs whether you choose one or more kinds of food and drinks for your guests. The four cooks all gained experience working in Asian countries and perfectly reproduce the enticing aromas and authentic flavors of Asian delicacies.

The prices are very reasonable, with courses starting from ¥3,500, including drinks. For fans of hot and spicy food we also recommend the ¥4,500 spicy course. All staff are English speaking for the comfort of all customers

NAZUKI

In addition to Japan being well known for its culinary delights, the country also enjoys a rich tradition of storytelling and folklore. Beautifully bringing the two together in a marriage of sensory bliss is Nazuki in the Tsukiji district of Tokyo. Entering the restaurant, guests descend an escalator swathed in a sublime blue light to find themselves in the largest restaurant space in Tsukiji. Boasting seating for 94, the main hall is awash is digital projection mapping that changes to fit any number of themes. With large aquariums teeming with tropical fish flanking the entrance, the walls, tables and even the floor come alive with images of coral reefs and graceful ocean life. The projections can be changed to suit the season, ranging from beautiful cherry trees in blossom in the spring and colorful fireworks in summer to vibrant fall foliage and snowy wonderlands in winter. The restaurant can also provide custom images for corporate events or wedding receptions.

Nazuki, in addition to the main dining hall, also has several private rooms that can accommodate from 2 to 44 people (when partitions are removed), allowing for more private functions. These rooms also boast digital projection mapping, offering diners a unique experience. People living in Tokyo are privileged to have the opportunity to experience both superb dining and traditional Japanese folktales in the heart of central Tokyo.

Although the restaurant offers various delicious lunch and dinner courses, the Story Course (¥8,500, tax included) is particularly popular with foreign residents and visitors alike. Offering dishes that represent scenes from popular traditional Japanese folktales, the fusion of food and fantasy is unprecedented in Tokyo. After images of the story are projected on the walls of the private room in a way that allows all members of one’s party to view them, food that has been cultivated to match the story arc is artfully presented to diners.

Rece

Located on a back street in Nihonbashi, rece is a hideaway Italian restaurant with an impressive cave-like entrance. The restaurant offers traditional Italian cuisine that brings out the best flavors of the high-quality ingredients. The chef, who trained in Florence for 7 years and worked at an Italian government official restaurant, carefully prepares each dish using carefully selected ingredients purchased via his chosen suppliers. Seasonal vegetables and fish, rare Japanese black beef from Iwate Prefecture, branded pork that rarely appears on the market, guinea fowl from Ibaraki and more are served on the monthly course.

Wine lovers will be attracted by rece’s large selection of wine. The restaurant offers a wide variety of wines from around the world, mainly from Italy, including rare vintages that are difficult to obtain. High-quality wine can be served by the bottle or by glass, so diners can try a variety with each dish expertly made by the chef. A very knowledgeable sommelier can pair wine to perfectly match to the meal. The restaurant has 12 seats where diners can savor authentic Italian cuisine in a cozy space that is perfect for business dinners, dates or special occasions.

Recommended is the Rece Course (¥9,350/with wine pairing, ¥15,500). This is a signature course named after the restaurant to enjoy seasonal ingredients and the chef's specialty. The course includes appetizers using carefully selected ingredients from fishing ports in Kyushu and Aomori, handmade pasta, main dishes such as A5-ranked Japanese black beef delivered directly from a farm in Iwate, short-horned beef that is difficult to obtain, guinea fowl from Ibaraki and more. Please enjoy various luxurious dishes with the freshest seasonal ingredients. The Assaggi Course (¥6600) offers diners 8 dishes, including a cold appetizer, warm appetizer, pasta, main dish and dessert, at a reasonable price.

Tokyo DisneySea

Tokyo DisneySea is a theme park at the Tokyo Disney Resort located in Urayasu, Chiba Prefecture, Japan, just outside Tokyo. It opened on 4 September 2001, at a cost of 335 billion yen. The Oriental Land Company owns the park, and licenses Disney characters and themes from The Walt Disney Company. With a size of 176-acre, Tokyo DisneySea attracted nearly fifteen million visitors in 2018, making it the fourth-most-visited theme park in the world.
